Lost Them , Found Me

BLOGS by ARCHITA JAISWAL — JUL 21, 2026

Crazy how your brain can hold onto a "maybe" long after reality has already said "no."

I don't even know why I kept that hope alive.

I had confessed.

He knew.

Nothing changed.

And yet, somewhere in the most delusional corner of my heart, there was this tiny voice whispering, "Maybe one day."

Maybe he'll realize.

Maybe he'll see me differently.

Maybe...

God, our brains are so embarrassing sometimes.

The funny part? We were actually good friends. Like, really good friends. We could talk for hours, laugh over the dumbest things, and somehow I convinced myself that this connection had to mean something more.

Spoiler alert: it didn't.

At least, not romantically.

And for the first time, I'm not writing this with a lump in my throat.

I'm writing this with... acceptance.

Not the fake "I'm over it" acceptance where you secretly stalk their Spotify playlists at 2 a.m.

The real kind.

The kind where your heart finally catches up with what your brain has known all along.

He doesn't like me that way.

And that's okay.

No, seriously.

It is.

Because why are we taught that if we love someone enough, they'll eventually love us back?

Who came up with that?

Love isn't a loyalty program where you collect enough kindness points and unlock someone's heart.

You don't get bonus points for caring harder.

People don't owe us love just because we loved them honestly.

That realization stings.

But it also sets you free.

For the longest time, I thought acceptance would feel like losing.

Turns out, it feels like putting down a bag you've been carrying for miles without realizing how heavy it was.

I'm no longer trying to decode every conversation.

No longer wondering if that smile meant something.

No longer writing imaginary future scenarios in my head after one nice interaction.

I'm tired.

And surprisingly... that's peaceful.

Here's something I've finally understood:

Someone can think you're amazing and still not want to date you.

Someone can genuinely care about you and still never fall in love with you.

Someone can choose to keep you in their life—but not in the role you were secretly hoping for.

That doesn't make them the villain.

And it definitely doesn't make you unlovable.

It just makes you two... different stories.

I think that's what growing up is.

Realizing that love isn't always mutual, closure doesn't always come with dramatic speeches, and sometimes the healthiest thing you can do is stop arguing with reality.

So this isn't a heartbreak post.

It's a graduation post.

Congratulations to me.

I have officially graduated from the University of "Maybe He'll Change His Mind."

No more delusions.

No more romanticizing breadcrumbs.

Just acceptance.

And if you're reading this while secretly waiting for someone to love you back...

I hope you know this:

Your love was never the embarrassing part.

Staying stuck in a place where your heart keeps asking for an answer it has already received—that's what hurts.

It's okay if they don't love you back.

Really.

Because one person's inability to love you romantically is not proof that you're hard to love.

Sometimes you're just not their person.

And maybe... they're not yours either.

I think that's the plot twist I was avoiding all along.

“There are still more beautiful chapters left to read."
