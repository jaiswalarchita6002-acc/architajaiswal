Half known , Half Hidden

BLOGS by ARCHITA JAISWAL — APR 12, 2026

It’s not like I don’t have friends. I do—actually, a lot.

But I don’t know why I still feel like I don’t.

And I know the ones reading this are my friends too.
And honestly, I have no intention of making you feel bad—no, that’s not what this is about.
This is just my space, where I write what I feel and choose to share it with you.

I do have people—people I share jokes and memes with, people I sit and eat with, people I laugh and rant with sometimes.
But still… all of it feels like just a filtered version of me.

And the weird part? Even I don’t fully understand why I do that.

I try—I really do—to bring the truest version of myself to the table.
But it’s just too difficult.

Not because I’m shy.
Not because I’m afraid.
But because my past experiences weren’t great.

And whether I like it or not, they’ve shaped the way I talk and interact with people now.

They’ve taught me to build boundaries.
To protect myself.
To think twice—sometimes ten times—before I say anything or do anything.

Because there have been moments when I actually tried to open up.
And all I got in return was being ignored…
or being called “too emotional.”

As if that’s an insult.

And you’d agree—everyone has that one person.
That one friend you can talk to about anything. Literally anything.

They don’t judge you for your stupid decisions.
They don’t cringe at your past or your trauma.
They don’t make you feel like you’re “too much.”

They just… stay.

They care.
They support.
They understand.
They listen, and they give you real advice—
because they love you
and they can’t stand seeing you hurt.

And I’ll admit it—
I haven’t found mine yet.

But it’s okay.

Life has just started.
There are so many days left, so many people left to meet.

I won’t lose hope.
I’ll keep building new bonds,
keep trying,
keep experiencing.

Because at the end of the day—
that’s what life is about, isn’t it?

“It’s not the absence of people that hurts, it’s the absence of being truly known and understood”
